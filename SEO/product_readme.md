# Pureleven Product Readme

This file is the quick execution guide for product discovery, article-to-product funneling, and conversion-oriented content updates after the recent live push.

## Current live follow-up items

1. Top images are taking too much space. Limit hero media to a small portion of the article and place it inside a framed card rather than using a large banner.
2. Every blog and article needs an upsell plan so informational traffic discovers products, collections, and best sellers.
3. Product-page routing needs to be deliberate so high-intent users can reach the relevant product page quickly.

## Image treatment standard

Use a restrained hero, not a giant banner.

- Keep article hero media within roughly `35-40vh` maximum height.
- Present the image inside a card, panel, or framed media block.
- Keep image width around `30-45%` of the content area when paired with text.
- Prefer small premium spice visuals, texture closeups, or farm-detail shots.
- Avoid oversized stock-style banners that push content below the fold.

## Product discovery standard for blogs and articles

Every article should behave like an SEO landing page plus a product funnel, not just an informational post.

Required discovery modules:

1. Hero CTA row with links to the primary collection and highest-intent hero product.
2. Quick comparison or summary block near the top.
3. Inline featured product cards every 2-3 major sections.
4. Featured products section near the lower half of the article.
5. Explore collections section before FAQ or near the close.
6. Related reading section to keep users inside the content ecosystem.
7. Final homepage or best-sellers CTA.

## Redirect and routing policy

Do not force timed redirects on strong SEO articles. That weakens user trust, harms dwell time, and can reduce search value.

Use this policy instead:

1. Evergreen ranking articles should stay indexable and use strong CTA routing to relevant product pages and collections.
2. Sticky desktop sidebar and sticky mobile CTA bars should send high-intent readers to product pages.
3. If an article becomes outdated, thin, or duplicative, use a clean `301` redirect to the closest product page, collection page, or newer replacement article.
4. Product-first landing articles can be written to route readers fast, but they should still provide enough content to stand as useful landing pages.
