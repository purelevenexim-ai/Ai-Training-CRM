## Section-by-section structure

1. Quick answer band
- Define both cardamom types immediately.
- State clearly that they are not direct substitutes.
- Add short decision cards for chai, desserts, biryani, and rich curries.

2. Human hook and guide framing
- Use a grounded pantry scene: opening green pods for chai versus crushing black cardamom for a slow-cooked dish.
- Keep it observational, not first-person travel storytelling.
- Add a short `What you'll learn` checklist in the side note.

3. Core comparison table
- Compare aroma, taste, appearance, best uses, cooking behavior, and storage.
- This is the main snippet-ready section.

4. When to use each type
- Explain green cardamom for chai, desserts, rice, and lighter dishes.
- Explain black cardamom for biryani, curries, gravies, and slow cooking.
- Keep the advice practical and kitchen-led.

5. Buying and storage block
- Show how whole-pod aroma, pod condition, and storage affect the buying decision.
- Reinforce that bigger size alone does not settle the quality question.

6. Kerala perspective section
- Make it explicit that the Pureleven buying path here is the green Kerala cardamom side of the comparison.
- Use Rajakumari, Idukki, Kerala as the grounded brand anchor.

7. Myth vs fact
- Correct common confusions such as `black cardamom is just older green cardamom` and `one always replaces the other`.

8. Short answers and FAQs
- Keep these concise and extractable.
- Use natural question phrasing.

9. Product discovery
- Route readers to Kerala green cardamom packs and the collection page.
- Do not imply a live black cardamom product if none is supplied.

10. Closing CTA
- Close with a simple buying framework based on what the reader cooks most often.
