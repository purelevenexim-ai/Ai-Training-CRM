## 2. PROBLEMS IDENTIFIED

### Problem 1: Interest Targeting Too Broad
- **Issue:** "Cooking" interest is extremely broad
- **Who it reaches:** General food bloggers, recipe enthusiasts, cooking show fans, meal prep people, restaurant critics
- **Who it MISSES:** Spice enthusiasts specifically looking for premium cardamom, pepper, etc.
- **Impact:** High impression volume but poor conversion quality
- **Evidence:** Industry data shows cooking audience converts at 2-4x lower rate than "specialty ingredients" audience

### Problem 2: Kerala-Only Targeting (Geographic Mismatch)
- **Issue:** "Traffic Campaign Cardamom Expats" explicitly designed to reach diaspora Indians
- **Current Reality:** Only targeting Kerala
- **Missing Markets:**
  - USA (Highest diaspora density: California, Texas, New Jersey, Illinois)
  - UK, Canada, UAE, Singapore, Australia
  - Second-tier Indian cities outside Kerala
- **Impact:** Misses 80%+ of intended "Expat" audience
- **Annual Revenue Loss:** Estimated $50K-100K+ from international customers not reached

### Problem 3: Large Audience Size (2.3M-2.7M)
- **Issue:** Audience is massive but poorly qualified
- **CPM Risk:** Large general audiences typically have higher CPM (₹400-600) due to low intent
- **Expected Performance:** 0.5-1.2 ROAS on current setup
- **Comparison:** Optimized spice-focused audience typically: 0.8-2.5 ROAS at ₹150-250 CPM
