2. Human hook and guide framing

- Keep it observational and grounded.

3. Core spice-quality checklist table
- Show what to check, why it matters, and what problems the signal can reveal.

4. Black pepper quality fold-in
- Add a dedicated section on whole peppercorn aroma, density, berry condition, and home-level quality clues.

5. Label and source guidance
- Explain why source clarity, pack date, and handling detail matter.

6. Household checks and adulteration cues
- Keep this practical and non-alarmist.

7. Pureleven insight and Kerala context
- Use the black-pepper product as the most concrete buying example.

8. Myth vs fact
- Correct common mistakes around color, shine, heaviness, and quick home tests.

9. Product discovery
- Route readers toward black pepper first, then cardamom and combo packs.

10. Closing CTA
- End with a repeatable buying framework instead of a hard sell.
