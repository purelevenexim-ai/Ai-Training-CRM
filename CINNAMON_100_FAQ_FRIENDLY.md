### Q6. What's Karuvappatta?
**Answer**: Oh, that's just the local Malayalam name for cinnamon stick! "Karuva" means dark and "Patta" means bark (the tree's skin basically). So Karuvappatta = the dark bark, which is our cinnamon. Same thing, different name!

**Malayalam**: കറുവപ്പട്ട = കറുവ (കറുത്ത) + പട്ട (വൃക്ഷത്തിന്റെ ചര്മ്മം). ഇത് സിലോൻ കറുവപ്പട്ടയാണ്.

**Answer**: Yeah, both work! Sticks are great for brewing tea or coffee because they release flavor slowly. Powder is better if you want instant flavor in food, drinks, or yogurt. Just remember that powder loses its smell faster, so sticks stay fresh way longer. We've got both!

**Answer**: Good instincts! Fresh cinnamon:

- **Flexible quills** — bends without cracking

- **Oily sheen** — you can see the essential oils
- **Check the date**: Look for packaging date on the pack!

### Q52. How long does cinnamon last?
**Answer**:

## SECTION 6: RECIPES & USAGE (Q66-Q80)

### Q66. How do I make cinnamon tea?
**Answer**: Super easy!
- **Method 1 (Stick)**: Boil 1 stick in 1 cup water for 5 mins, then steep 10 mins, strain. Add honey and lemon if you like!
- **Method 2 (Powder)**: ¼ teaspoon powder in hot water, mix well, add honey, done!
- **Reuse the stick**: One stick makes like 5-7 cups (good for 2-3 days)
- **Best time**: Morning for digestion, evening for better sleep
- **Benefit**: Helps digestion, reduces inflammation
