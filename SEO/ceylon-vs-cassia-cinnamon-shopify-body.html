Quick Answer

Ceylon and cassia cinnamon solve different kitchen problems

Ceylon cinnamon is lighter, sweeter, and more delicate, so it suits tea, desserts, fruit, and recipes where you want finesse. Cassia cinnamon is warmer, bolder, and more direct, so it fits chai, stronger baking, breakfast bowls, and savory spice work where cinnamon should show up clearly.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Ceylon and cassia cinnamon

What is the main difference between Ceylon and cassia cinnamon?

Ceylon is lighter and sweeter, while cassia is warmer, bolder, and more direct in flavor.

Which cinnamon is better for tea?

Ceylon is often the better fit for tea when you want a gentler, cleaner cinnamon profile.

Which cinnamon is better for baking?

Cassia is often better when you want a stronger cinnamon note in cakes, cookies, and breakfast bakes.

Can I keep both types in the kitchen?

Yes. Many kitchens benefit from having both because they solve different flavor problems.

How should cinnamon be stored?

Keep it sealed, dry, and away from heat and direct light so the aroma stays cleaner for longer.
