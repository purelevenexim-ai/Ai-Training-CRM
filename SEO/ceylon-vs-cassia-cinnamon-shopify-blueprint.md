## Upgrade goals

1. Give buyers one answer-first comparison instead of making them infer the difference from two product pages.
2. Support both cinnamon products without forcing a winner narrative.
3. Explain dish fit clearly: softer Ceylon versus bolder cassia.
4. Convert cinnamon comparison traffic into cleaner product selection.

## Section structure

1. Quick answer band
- Define both cinnamon styles.
- State where each one wins.

2. Human hook
- Compare how the two behave in tea, baking, and savory cooking.

3. Comparison table
- Flavor, aroma, use case, intensity, and buyer fit.

4. Best uses
- Tea, desserts, fruit, and lighter baking for Ceylon.
- Chai, stronger baking, breakfast bowls, and savory spice work for cassia.

5. Buying guidance
- Help readers choose based on cooking habits rather than abstract prestige.

6. Pureleven insight
- Connect directly to the two live cinnamon products.

7. FAQ block
- Focus on everyday substitution and use cases.

8. Soft CTA
- Route readers to both product pages.
